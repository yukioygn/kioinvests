EWBC — Full price history

01

The
Panic

In March 2023, Silicon Valley Bank collapsed in 48 hours. Signature Bank followed. First Republic teetered. The contagion was real — but it was also specific. SVB had loaded its balance sheet with long-duration bonds while rates rose, and served a concentrated depositor base of venture-backed companies all wired into the same panic network. When confidence broke, it broke fast. The market, unwilling to distinguish, sold every regional bank in sight. EWBC fell sharply alongside institutions that shared nothing with SVB except a zip code in the same regulatory category. The selloff was indiscriminate, providing ample opportunity.

02

The
Distinction

EastWest Bancorp was not SVB. Founded to serve the Chinese-American community, it had grown into a full-service commercial bank with a structural moat that most regional banks could not claim — one of the only U.S. institutions holding a full banking license in China. Its deposit base was retail-anchored and relationship-driven. Its balance sheet was conservatively managed. Its profitability was industry-leading. The position was initiated on May 22, 2023, at $49.14. The market had confused category with quality. EastWest's moat was intact. Its depositors had not run. The price was wrong.

03

The
Harvest

Each sale came reluctantly. EWBC was not a deteriorating business afterall; it was a compounding one. A thesis built on mispricing has a natural endpoint: the moment the price is no longer wrong. Each tranche tested that question freshly. When the original margin of safety had been recaptured and the repricing had substantially corrected, the remaining upside belonged to a different investor with a different thesis. The blended exit across all tranches was approximately $81.38 — a total return of 65.6% over roughly two years, annualized at approximately 28.6%. The timing of the exit was a judgment call, one we can live with.
