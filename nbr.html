NBR — Full price history

01

The
Burden

Nabors was a company the market had given up on. Its all-time high was over $1,200 per share. By early 2025 it traded at $30 given a decade of debt accumulation. Industry downturns and dwindling faith compressed the stock into something the investment community filed away under uninvestable. The bear case was simple and not wrong: the balance sheet was the risk. Although the bears were pricing the liability, management was taking painful and deliberate steps to dismantle the debt overhang. Net debt had peaked. The direction had changed. The market wasn't buying it.

02

The
Kingdom

The anchor of the thesis was SANAD: a 50/50 joint venture with Saudi Aramco, progressively deploying 50 newbuild rigs over a decade structured to recover invested capital within five years. This was not optionality. It was contracted, long-duration, capital-recovering revenue from the most consequential oil producer on earth. Each new rig was a guaranteed cash flow unit coming online. Geopolitical risk in the region was the very reason Saudi Arabia's production expansion was non-negotiable. The Kingdom needed the rigs. Nabors had the contract. We had the preparation and conviction to buy.

03

The
Reinvention

This is not the Nabors of 2014. The company that once competed on rig count alone was building a proprietary software and automation layer on top of its hardware base. SmartDRILL, SmartSLIDE, RigCLOUD, Canrig robotics point to a business model in transition. In 2025, Nabors and Halliburton completed the first fully automated land drilling operations in Oman. In April 2025, an AI analytics partnership with Corva expanded real-time predictive capabilities across the fleet. The market was pricing a leveraged rig operator. What was being built was something closer to an energy technology platform.
