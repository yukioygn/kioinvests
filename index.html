Investment Philosophy
01

Variant
Perception

The market prices consensus. Edge lives in the gap between what is widely believed and what is actually true. Every position begins with one question: what do we see that others don't — and why are we right?

02

Patient
Capital

Time is the mechanism through which the market corrects its own errors. We do not trade. We own. The willingness to hold through discomfort — while others capitulate — is not a temperamental quirk. It is the strategy.

03

Asymmetric
Risk

The goal is never to avoid risk. It is to find situations where the downside is bounded and the upside is not. Where heads you win considerably, and tails you don't lose much. That asymmetry, patiently sought, is the only edge worth having.
