BABA — Full price history

01

The
Crackdown

In October 2020, Jack Ma criticized China's financial regulators at a Shanghai summit. Within weeks the $37 billion Ant Group IPO was halted, an antitrust investigation into Alibaba was launched, and the likable frontman of Alibaba vanished from public view. What followed was a multi-year regulatory siege in the form of fines, restructuring mandates, and data governance rules that erased more than $600 billion in market value from Chinese tech at its peak. The stock fell from over $300 to under $70. The market concluded the story was over.

02

The
Distinction

It was not. Alibaba remained the dominant infrastructure layer of Chinese commerce — e-commerce, cloud, logistics, payments — with no credible domestic challenger. The regulatory crackdown was painful, but it was political, not structural. China at its core is a pro-business regime. It needs its private sector. The question was never whether the relationship would normalize — it was when. For a long-term investor, that question did not matter. The position was initiated on May 20, 2024, at $87.56 — at all-time lows. Peak fear had created the entry.

03

The
Pivot

The AI development was not the thesis, but a windfall bonus. Alibaba's Qwen launched publicly in September 2023 and by 2025 had become one of the most downloaded open-source model series in the world. In February 2025, Jack Ma appeared at a meeting with President Xi Jinping alongside China's leading entrepreneurs: a visible signal of rehabilitation. A partial position was exited on September 17, 2025 at $166.42. The remaining position reflects continued conviction that the repricing of Alibaba's full value has not yet run its course.
