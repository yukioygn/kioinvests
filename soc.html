01

The
Perception

Sable Offshore was not a broken business. It was a business caught in political limbo — and there is a meaningful difference between the two. The Santa Ynez Unit had been shut in since 2015, not because the oil was gone, but because California's regulatory apparatus had made restart effectively impossible. By late 2025 the market had stopped distinguishing between a broken asset and a blocked one. The real question was never operational. It was political. A change in administration would bring a change in energy philosophy — and California's decade-long blockade was precisely the kind of target a drill-baby-drill executive would want to dismantle. The market was solving the wrong equation.

02

The
Decision

The position was initiated on November 24, 2025, at $4.77 per share. Six weeks prior, a California judge had issued an injunction blocking pipeline restart, sending shares down 26% in a single session. The market read this as confirmation that California had won. The stock fell until the sellers ran out. That exhaustion was the entry point. The balance sheet was distressed. The regulatory outlook was hostile. In practice, this looked like a call option on a political outcome — with a defined downside and an open-ended upside the market was not pricing. The prior all-time high of $35 was not a ceiling. It was a reference point for what the market had already been willing to pay when the thesis looked viable.

03

The
Reckoning

The thesis proved correct. The mechanism exceeded expectations. On March 13, 2026 — 110 days after entry — Energy Secretary Wright invoked the Defense Production Act, directing Sable to restore pipeline operations immediately. Oil began flowing the following day. A partial position was exited at $15.13 on May 15, 2026 — a return of approximately +218% in six months. California has not conceded. The legal battle between federal authority and state sovereignty remains active and unresolved. The remaining position reflects continued conviction in the underlying asset. The original call option has been partially exercised. What happens next demands fresh eyes.
