GOOGL — Full price history

01

The
Misread

When DeepSeek dropped in January 2025, the reaction was not measured skepticism — it was panic. Investors, media, and retail traders converged on a single conclusion: Google was finished. Search was dead. ChatGPT had eaten the future, and a Chinese startup had now proven frontier AI could be built for pennies. The fear was visceral. It was also wrong. The market was confusing the model race with the distribution war — two entirely different competitions. Google's moat was never built on having the best model. It was built on three billion Android devices, two billion Search users, and the world's largest video platform all belonging to the same company. You cannot replicate that with a cheaper model. The market was pricing existential threat. The moat was intact.

02

The
Entry

The position was initiated on March 7, 2025, at $175 — before Liberation Day, before the April selloff pushed the stock lower still. The DeepSeek panic had already compressed the price sharply from late 2024 highs. February earnings rattled investors further: cloud revenue missed marginally while capex guidance came in well above expectations. The market read heavy spending as recklessness. The alternative reading was simpler — Google Cloud was growing above 30 percent year-on-year, the contracted backlog was accumulating faster than infrastructure could be built, and a company raising capital aggressively does so because demand is outrunning supply. At $175, the market was pricing peak fear. The business was pricing a supercycle.

03

The
Platform

What the market consistently underweights in Alphabet is the compounding nature of its data advantage. YouTube is an irreplaceable training resource that OpenAI and its peers cannot access, purchase, or replicate. Google Search indexes the web in real time. Gmail and Docs sit inside the daily workflows of three billion people. The AI war is not won in a single model release. It is won over years of compounding data, infrastructure, and integration, precisely the terrain where Alphabet has no equal. Google Cloud reached a $70 billion annual run rate with growth accelerating to 63 percent in Q1 2026. No one could have predicted the success of Gemini and NotebookLM a few years ago, but they were still betting against Google.
